User Guide

Transcripts

Viewing your transcripts

Transcripts can be found in the transcripts tab in your dashboard. There is a filterable list of all of your transcripts. You can search for a transcript by the name of the student or by the years of attendence. Just enter your criteria and click the Search button.
To view all of your transcripts again, click the Reset button.

Creating a new transcript

Transcripts are created from the list of transcripts in your dashboard. Click the + Create Transcript button to add a new transcript.

The new transcript form will open and you can start filling in your student's information.

The transcript form has four pages: Student, School, Academics, and Signature.

The Student and School pages are where you enter the contact information for your student and your school. To enter your address, first select your country from the dropdown and then click + Add Address. For US addresses, a standard address form will be shown. For addresses outside the US, a textbox will be shown. You should enter your address like it would appear on an envelope.

The Academics page is where you enter the courses your student completed during each year of enrollment and the grades and credits that were awarded for each course. This is the most complex step, so we'll explain how it works in more detail.

The Academics page is broken down into four years. The years are arranged in chronological order from Freshman to Senior. To add a course to a school year, click the + Add Another Row button under the year that course was taken.

Each course has a title, category, type, grade, and credits. The title is the name of the course. The category is the category or subject the course falls under, e.g. English or Math. The type of the course marks whether it was an honors or AP course. The grade field holds the letter grade the student received for the course (see below on how letter grades become grade point averages). The credits fields indicates how many highschool credits the course is worth.

Most US states and universities have requirements on how many credits a highschool course is worth.
With the exception of Indiana, Idaho, New Jersey, and California, a highscool course which lasts one year or 120 hours of instruction is generally worth one credit.

The Signature page allows you to enter information about who certifies that the information in the transcript is accurate. You may enter their title, the date the transcript was signed and optionally upload an image of their signature. There is also a notes field for any information that doesn't fit into the rest of the transcript.

Editing a transcript

You can open a transcript for editing by clicking the name of the student in the list of transcripts or by clicking the (edit) icon in its row.

The transcript edit form will open and you can update the information in the transcript.
If you need to get to a particular page quickly, click the icon for that page at the top of the form.

To change the country for your address, select the correct country from the dropdown and then click Change Country

Deleting a transcript

You can delete a transcript by clicking the icon to the left of the transcript you would like to delete.
Because deleting a transcript could cause you to lose a lot of work, you will need to confirm that you want to delete the transcript.
Once a transcript is deleted there is no guarantee it can be restored.

Grading

Default Grading Scale

The default grading scale uses a four point system with letter grades from A+ to F. Honors and AP courses have their grades weighted. Honors courses can receive a maximum of 4.5 points and AP courses can receive a maximum of 5 points. Half credit courses can receive a maximum of 2 points.

Custom Grading Scales

If you use a grading system that is not based on a 4 point system or A through F letter grades, you can use a custom grading scale. Grading scales can be created from the Grading Scales tab in your dashboard. Simply click the + New Grading Scale button.

Each grading scale has a name, and a set of grade increments. Grade increments are added by clicking the + Add Grade Increment button when editing the grading scale.
You must name and save your grading scale before you can add an increment

Each grade increment has a letter grade and a GPA value. The letter grade is what you will put for the grade when entering courses on the transcript, and the GPA value is what will be used to calculate your student's GPA.

If you need to include honors or AP courses and wish to use a weighted version of your custom grading scale, you will need to add a separate, weighted version of your scale for each.

You may set the grading scale each transcript uses at the top of the Academics page.

Printing Transcripts

One page transcript

The print version of your transcript is formatted to save as much space as possible. It can fit a maximum of 15 courses per year on a single page.

International Addresses

Addresses outside the United States are displayed as entered with the country added as the last line.

Print settings

The print settings are displayed whenever you print your transcript. Your transcript can be printed from the Signature page of the transcript editor or by clicking the icon in the list of transcripts in your dashboard.

Organize courses by subject

You may print your transcripts with the courses organized by by subject. This format will organize courses using their category field. Any course without a value in the category field will be placed in the "Other" category. This setting may be turned on in the Print Settings before printing your transcript.

Unweighted GPA

This setting may be turned on in the Print Settings before printing your transcript. It removes weighting from Honors and AP courses and grades them the same as normal high school courses.

Account

Updating account information

You can update your name and email address on the Account tab in your dashboard. To change your password, click the Change Password link and fill out the form.

Subscription Status

The status of your subscription is displayed on the Account tab of your dashboard.
